CULTURE AND TRADITIONAL

Traditional Clothing

Dubai's Islamic traditions and heritage of culture are reflected in its traditional clothing. Men dress in a white headpiece called a Gutra and a white ankle-length robe called a Kandura or Dishdasha. They may wear a Bisht on important events. Women dress in the Hijab, Shyla, Gishwah, Kaftan, Jalabiya, and the Abaya, a loose-fitting black garment. These clothes highlight modesty and offer protection from the wind and sun of the desert. Many Emiratis proudly dress their traditional clothing as a representation of their culture and identity.

Foods

Dubai's food scene combines foreign cuisines with traditional Emirati dishes. In addition to popular meals like machboos, luqaimat, and harees, the native cuisine comprises of rice, meat, fish, and dates. Indian, Pakistani, Mediterranean, and Asian cuisines are all represented in Dubai's varied eating selections. While high-end restaurants serve elegant takes on classic meals, street food sellers provide more reasonably priced options like falafel and shawarma.

Celebrations

Eidul Fitri and Eidul Adha. The Festival of Sacrifice, or Eidul Adha, honors Prophet Ibrahim's willingness to offer his son as a sacrifice. The Qurbani tradition, which involves slaughtering animals and distributing meat to needy households, comes after special prayers at mosques. Locals cook holiday dinners that include classic food like baklava and lamb biryani. Ramadan fasting ends on Eidul Fitri. Community prayers, donations to charities, and happy gatherings with loved ones are all part of the celebrations.
